Patient Care

We are a private practice dedicated to providing dental care in a relaxed, unhurried environment — your well-being is our number one priority.

Our Commitment

Care You Can Trust

We are a private practice, but also able to provide NHS dental and orthodontic care to children under 18, or aged 18 and in full-time education. Our dental care is carried out in a relaxed manner with your comfort and well-being of paramount importance.

With all staff constantly undertaking continuous professional development, our caring and knowledgeable team ensure you benefit from the latest improvements in dentistry.

  • Highest hygiene & cross-infection standards
  • All staff trained in medical emergencies
  • On-site defibrillator and emergency kit
  • GDPR-compliant computerised records
  • No practice membership or joining fees
Getting Started

New Patients Welcome

We are pleased to welcome new patients and their families to our practice.

Step 1 — Register

Your new patient journey begins with our friendly reception team collecting your personal details and medical history.

Step 2 — Examination

A comprehensive first examination where we assess your oral health, perform a smile assessment, and check for any issues including signs of mouth cancer.

Step 3 — Your Plan

We discuss your oral health, any recommended treatments, and provide a written treatment plan. We arrange future appointments to suit your schedule.

Adults are seen on a private basis. Children under 18, or those aged 18 and in full-time education, may be seen under the NHS free of charge. For more details, feel free to contact us.

Urgent Care

Emergency Dental Care

If you are experiencing a dental emergency, please call us as early in the day as possible so we can do our best to see you. We keep appointment slots available each day for urgent cases.

For existing patients, please call 01453 828327 first thing in the morning. For dental emergencies outside our opening hours, please contact NHS 111 for advice.

  • Toothache and acute pain
  • Dental trauma and broken teeth
  • Lost or broken fillings/crowns
  • Dental abscesses and swelling
